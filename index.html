Introduction

Where I'm From

I am from Sea Girt NJ, a small, literally one square mile, town at the Jersey Shore. It’s a great place to escape from city life to go to the beach, ride a bike or take a walk in a beautiful spot. At the entrance to town there is a big sign that states, “Smile, You’re in Sea Girt.” It’s home to the The Parker House, a pretty famous summer bar. In fact, the New York Times suggested it beats the Hamptons. There is also a great Italian specialty store, Joe Leones, that makes the most delicious mozzarella sandwiches in the world.

What are your favorite hobbies?

I am an avid reader who enjoys escaping into a story. It fun being engrossed in the lives of various characters. Sometimes they “almost” feel like friends. I read a variety of books and I’m in a book club. I’m also a dancer and regularly enjoy Zumba classes with friends. Zumba is a 60-minute cardio dance party which is so much fun you forget you’re working out. I recently started doing yoga regularly which is good for stretching and balance. I also enjoy the challenge of a large jigsaw puzzle and usually have one in progress on my coffee table.

What's your dream job?

I have a degree in Communications, but my background presents a very general set of skills. I always knew I needed to find my niche, a particular set of skills to market to employers. I am excited to enter a field with lots of room for learning, growth and ample job opportunities. I am still investigating specific job possibilities and seeking out companies whose work I value. I am certainly also considering the practical, salary and good benefits.

Where do you live?

I live in NYC on the Upper West Side. I love living here because there’s always something fun to do, usually right in the neighborhood. It’s great that everything is just a short walk from home. Central Park is nearby which is the perfect place to enjoy nature. I’m a foodie, so I love exploring various restaurants with friends. Some of my favorites include The Ribbon, Sweet Greens, Tacombi and Magnolia Bakery for a sweet treat. On the weekends I always visit the local farm market to get fresh fruits and veggies for the week and stop by Shakespeare & Co., the local bookstore and café, for my favorite tea and to just peruse.

Why do you want to be a web developer?

This past month I introduced myself to the field by taking an entry level class with SheCodes. The class covered HTML, CSS and Javascript. I found learning these languages to be fun and engaging. I believe coding plays to my strengths. I really like how VsCode shows the code in color. For me this makes it pleasant on the eye and helpful in finding errors. I really like that coding is project oriented. I have always enjoyed very structured work.
